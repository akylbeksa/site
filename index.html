The Problem

Many cities suffer from “ghost projects”: perfectly fine streets get renovated while nearby areas require more attention. Decisions are often subjective, slow, and hard to verify.

Subjectivity & corruption risk

Budget allocation is often decided by people, not by objective infrastructure condition data.

Ineffective repair work

Repairs are either reactive (after incidents) or unnecessary (renovating already-good areas).

Lack of transparency for the public

Citizens can’t verify whether a street truly needed a multi-million renovation.

The Solution

antiBAD turns camera footage into actionable infrastructure development solutions: where the situation is bad, how bad it is, and what should be funded first.

1

Scanning

Processes video recordings from existing city CCTV networks or mobile cameras for continuous scanning of infrastructure.

2

AI Diagnosis

Computer Vision (YOLOv8 / segmentation) detects potholes, cracks, and erosion and assigns a Severity Score (0-1).

3

Verification

Identifies areas that truly require repair, avoiding unnecessary spending.

FAQ

Short answers to common questions from municipalities and stakeholders.

What makes antiBAD different from typical monitoring systems?

antiBAD connects infrastructure condition to budgeting decisions. It prioritizes repairs based on evidence and flags unjustified renovation requests.

Do we need to install new cameras?

No. The goal is to use existing CCTV networks and/or mobile cameras where available.

How do you validate model quality?

We use standard metrics (precision/recall), district-based testing, and calibration per camera type, lighting, and environment. Outputs can be logged for auditability.

Cloud or on‑prem?

Both are possible. Deployment depends on local security, compliance, and integration requirements.
